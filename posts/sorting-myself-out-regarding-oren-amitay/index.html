Sorting Myself Out - regarding Oren Amitay

December 29, 2020 5-minute read
sensemaking • education

This is some of my notes in preparation for a conversation with Grizwald and Mercury.

Acknowledgement (TCP protocol)

  • My wife helps me so much. She is amazing. I appreciate her so much.
  • Don’t be superficial - think critically - question beliefs. This is what attracted my wife to me.
  • My wife asks me - what good are you doing?
  • Magnesium helped me. My wife discovered magnesium for me.
  • Dreams have helped me. I think I learned empathy in a dream. I thought the feeling of unconditional love was nirvana.
  • Sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic.
  • We can create our own story.

Oren

He doesn’t mention the word compassion or love even though he is called “doctor” and all of the diagnosis for mental health problems are related to love either directly or indirectly. - Gavin

  • He talks about laws related to diagnosing people.
    • Laws which prevent practicing medicine without a license.
    • Laws which restrict the way a doctor practices medicine.
    • Ultimately - I uphold compassion and education.
      • Is it compassionate to allow someone to kill themselves?
  • He talks about conspiracy theory and conspiracy fact. The facts can be true but how do you connect the dots?
    • I started seeing conspiracy theories on a more practical level by remembering what my wife asked me - what good are you doing?
    • If believing in the story helps produce good then it is a good story. Religious people have a story - a conspiracy theory.
    • What is a good story that we can imagine together?
  • He talks about someone jumping off a balcony but the family said the police pushed her.
    • This is a case where people are quick to believe a story and they aren’t thinking about whether or not the belief produces good outcomes.
    • The “true true” is a story that is true and good. It would be true to Jordan Peterson and to Sam Harris. They use the word in different ways.
  • He is OK living in uncertainty and he will do his best if there is something he can do to help.
    • I have a similar view. But there is an important question.
    • What can I do to help?
      • What is the story we are creating?
  • He talks about nobody being a stay at home parent.
    • I didn’t have a stay at home parent.
    • The more important issue is whether or not the parent is listening to their child and how often. Many parents do not listen to their child and live a superficial life.
  • When his child looks up his child will see him looking into their eyes - not on a screen.
    • When I looked up, my parents weren’t there. My father wanted to raise me so that I didn’t get disappointed if I was doing good work and nobody was watching.
    • Do good work even if nobody acknowledges it.
  • He got in trouble on Twitter for using the wrong words.
    • I have not punished my children for using the wrong words.
    • I don’t plan on punishing my children for using the wrong words.
    • I teach my children about the risk of using the wrong words in our sick world.
  • So many people’s problems start because they don’t feel part of a group.
    • But he wore “being the other” as a “badge of honor”.
    • I do enjoy the privilege of having family and friends that I can ask for help.
    • I try to be a person that others can ask for help. But I also try to be careful about this.
      • I am a helper and a volunteer.

Grizwald

  • Grizwald talks about QAnon and how they view Trump as a hero.
    • I believe I would be a better leader than Trump.
    • The danger of a hero is the idea that you can forfeit personal responsibility.
  • Grizwald says there is nothing for young men to aspire to in our society anymore.
    • We need to recruit people who want to be a part of the story which we imagine together.
  • Grizwald says he goes and plays NPC for 8 hours a day.
    • When I do my job - I am developing my skills stack.
  • Grizwald says when there are people in your life that you can touch - touch them - give them a hug.
    • “Everybody needs to hug somebody - at least once a day” - Franti
  • Grizwald hopes we will try communes.
    • Oren says communes fail after a certain number of people. If you can contain size and select who is in it then it can work. Oren speaks against narcissists and psychopaths - you don’t want them in your commune.
    • We can build intentional communities. But we need to figure out the process by which we do hiring, firing, promotion, and demotion. The human resource problem is real.
  • Grizwald says ready player one is a dystopian nightmare world.
    • For some people it is not.
    • We can imagine the technology used for good.
  • Grizwald says challenging the orthodoxy puts your job and livelihood at risk.
    • There are tactics and strategies which we can learn.

Ideas

  • People are trying to find some kind of purpose or meaning.
    • What good are you doing?
    • We must learn with others.
    • We must solve problems.
    • Some projects are better than others.
      • Missile Defense
      • Particle colliders
      • Space elevators
      • Ending hunger
  • Find something you can control and maintain your relationships.
    • Some people need a garden.
    • Be willing to have disagreements.
  • It’s important to believe someone has your back.
    • I disagree.
    • We need mutually beneficial relationships.
  • The mouse utopia experiments may warn us about some ideas.
    • The struggle and challenge is good.
    • Life without challenge is meaningless.
  • The issue of women entering the workforce.
    • I was raised by a single father.
  • Turn off screens and share a meal with your family.
    • Sometimes people aren’t present even though there is no screen.
    • People need a growth mindset founded on compassion (the holy spirit).
